Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our program

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?

Applicants must have a bachelor's degree with at least 50% marks from a recognized university. While any graduate can apply, preference is given to those with healthcare background or work experience in healthcare settings. GMAT/CAT scores are recommended but not mandatory.

How is the program structured?

The 2-year program is divided into four semesters. The first year covers foundation and core business subjects, while the second year focuses on healthcare specialization. The final semester includes a capstone project and industry internship.

Are scholarships available?

Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 50% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are available. Healthcare professionals returning to education may qualify for special scholarships.

What kind of placement support is provided?

Our dedicated Career Development Cell offers comprehensive placement support including resume building, interview preparation, and networking opportunities. We conduct a healthcare-specific placement week with 30+ recruiters visiting campus. Our 96% placement rate reflects our strong industry connections.

Is work experience required for admission?

Work experience is preferred but not mandatory. Fresh graduates with strong academic backgrounds are also considered. However, candidates with relevant healthcare experience may have an advantage in the selection process.

Can I specialize in a specific healthcare sector?

Yes, in the third semester, students can choose electives from specialized tracks including Hospital Administration, Health Insurance & Financing, Pharmaceutical Management, or Health Tech & Innovation. Your capstone project and internship can also be aligned with your specialization interest.

What is the fee payment schedule?

Fees are payable semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options including semester-wise, annual, or one-time full payment with applicable discounts.

What are the international opportunities available?

We have exchange programs with healthcare management schools in the US, UK, Singapore, and Australia. Students can opt for international internships, and our global alumni network helps in overseas placements. About 15% of our graduates work internationally within 3 years of graduation.
